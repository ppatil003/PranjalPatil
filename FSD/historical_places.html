Discover Pune's Historical Gems

  • Shaniwar Wada

    Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification in Pune. Built in 1732, it was the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818.

  • Aga Khan Palace

    The Aga Khan Palace is a majestic building built in 1892. It holds great historical significance as it served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters during the Indian independence movement.

  • Pataleshwar Cave Temple

    Pataleshwar Cave Temple is a rock-cut cave temple dating back to the 8th century. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a protected monument under the Archaeological Survey of India.

  • Lal Mahal

    Lal Mahal was originally established in 1630 AD by Shivaji Maharaj's father, Shahaji Bhosale. It was one of Shivaji's childhood residences and an important site in Maratha history.

  • Vishrambaug Wada

    Vishrambaug Wada is a fine mansion located in Pune. It was the luxurious residence of the Peshwa Baji Rao II and is known for its beautiful architecture.

  • Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum

    This museum is dedicated to the memory of Raja Dinkar Kelkar, showcasing a collection of various artifacts that reflect the everyday life of Indians in the past centuries.
